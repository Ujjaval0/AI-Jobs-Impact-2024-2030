## Dashboard 1 — The Startup World

![Startup Landscape Dashboard](./dashboard1_startup.png)

**500 startups · 8 industries · 5 global regions**

### What the data found:

- **AI has the worst return on investment** of all 8 industries — $7.90 created per $1 invested vs E-Commerce at $10.00. The loudest industry delivers the least value per dollar.

- **76% of AI and Gaming startups never exit** — no IPO (Initial Public Offering), no acquisition. Despite all the hype, most AI startups stay private forever.

- **FinTech gets acquired the most** — 1 in 3 FinTech startups gets bought out. Best industry if acquisition is your goal.

- **Startup funding is genuinely global now** — Asia, Australia, Europe, North America, South America are all within 2% of each other. Silicon Valley no longer controls the money.

## Dashboard 2 — Real Funding Deals (2024–25)

**25 real companies · $10.7B raised · 7 countries**

*SpaceX, OpenAI, Revolut, Anthropic, Klarna — companies you know.*

### What the data found:

- **India raises more per deal than the USA** — $745M average vs $403M. Nearly double. India had only 3 startups here but each one raised massive amounts.

- **60% of all funding went to NON-AI companies** — The 3 biggest deals were SpaceX ($1.5B), Byju's ($1.2B), and OpenAI ($1B). Two of three had nothing to do with AI.

- **One country beats four combined** — USA raised $6.04B. India raised $2.24B. All of Europe (UK + France + Germany + Sweden) raised $2.19B. India alone outperformed the entire continent.

- **FinTech matched AI dollar for dollar** — Pure AI raised $2,435M. FinTech raised $2,425M. A $10M difference — with zero media coverage.

## Dashboard 3 — Jobs in the Age of AI (2024–2030)

**30,000 job titles · 8 industries · real projections to 2030**

### Job Growth by Industry

| Industry | Change by 2030 | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| IT | +511,824 | 🔵 Growing fast |
| Healthcare | +403,323 | 🔵 Growing |
| Entertainment | +386,550 | 🔵 Growing |
| Retail | +221,177 | 🔵 Growing |
| Finance | +39,182 | 🔵 Slowly |
| Manufacturing | +12,474 | ⚪ Nearly flat |
| Education | -92,910 | 🔴 Shrinking |
| Transportation | -444,302 | 🔴 Biggest loser |

### What the data found:

- **Transportation loses more jobs than manufacturing** — nearly half a million by 2030. Self-driving vehicles and automated logistics are the reason. Most people guessed this wrong.

- **4,437 jobs are already in the danger zone** — automation risk above 70% AND already declining today. Not a future problem — a right now problem.

- **4,520 jobs are completely safe** — roles needing real human judgment, creativity, and physical presence. AI cannot replace these.

- **Salary tells you nothing about job security** — IT earns $90,942/year. Transportation earns $89,451/year. A $1,491 gap between the safest and most endangered industry. High pay ≠ safe career.

## Recommendations

### 👨‍🎓 For Students & Job Seekers
- Go toward **IT or Healthcare** — most new jobs, highest pay, lowest automation risk
- Build skills that need **human judgment, creativity, physical presence** — these are the 4,520 safe jobs
- If you are in a repetitive role — start reskilling now, the 4,437 danger zone jobs are already shrinking

### 🏢 For Businesses
- **Do not chase AI just because it is trending** — E-Commerce and HealthTech deliver better returns
- **FinTech** offers the highest acquisition probability for founders seeking exits
- Transportation and Education businesses need to **restructure now**, not in 2028
