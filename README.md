> **Author:** Ujjaval Bhardwaj · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ujjaval-bhardwaj/)  
> **Period Covered:** 2024 – 2030

## 📌 Project Overview

Everyone has an opinion about AI. This project skips the opinions and goes straight to the data — **500 startups, 25 real funding deals, and 30,000 job titles** analyzed across 8 industries and 5 global regions.

What we found challenges almost everything people assume about AI, money, and careers.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Startups Analyzed | 500 |
| Funding Tracked | $10.7B |
| Job Titles Analyzed | 30,000 |
| Industries Covered | 8 |
| Global Regions | 5 |

**Tech Stack:** `React` · `Recharts` · `Lucide Icons` · `Vite` · `Vanilla CSS`  
**Data Work:** `Microsoft Excel` (cleaning) · `Power BI` (dashboards) · `DAX` (custom calculations)

---

## 🔴 The Surprising Findings (TL;DR)

> Things the data revealed that most people would not expect:

- 🔴 **AI has the worst ROI** of all startup industries — $7.9x vs E-Commerce at $10x
- ⚫ **76% of AI startups never exit** — they stay private forever
- 🟢 **India beats the USA** on average deal size per startup
- 🔴 **Transportation loses more jobs than Manufacturing** by 2030 — almost nobody sees this coming
- 🟢 **FinTech matched AI in total funding** within $10M — with zero headlines
- 🟢 **All 5 global regions are within 2% of each other** — geography barely matters for startup funding anymore
- 🔴 **Only $1,491/year salary gap** separates the safest and most endangered industry

## 📊 Dashboard 1 — The Startup World

> **Dataset:** 500 startups · 8 industries · 5 global regions  
> **Cleaned in Excel · Visualized in Power BI**

### 💡 Insight 01 — Most startups never exit, and that is completely normal

An **"exit"** means a startup either goes public (IPO) or gets acquired. Most startups do neither — they just keep building privately.

| Exit Type | Rate | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Stay Private | **76%** | Especially AI & Gaming startups |
| Get Acquired | **33%** | FinTech gets bought the most |
| Go Public (IPO) | **12%** | HealthTech has the best IPO rate |

> 🔍 76% of startups — across every industry — never exit. That's not failure. That's the norm.

---

### 💡 Insight 02 — The most efficient industry is NOT AI

**Efficiency** = for every $1 invested, how much company value does it create?

| Rank | Industry | Value per $1 Invested |
|---|---|---|
| 🥇 #1 | E-Commerce | **$10.0** |
| #2 | HealthTech | $9.8 |
| #3 | FinTech | $9.5 |
| #4 | Gaming | $9.2 |
| #5 | Aerospace | $8.8 |
| #6 | EdTech | $8.4 |
| #7 | SaaS | $8.1 |
| 🔴 Last | **AI** | **$7.9** |

> ⚠️ The loudest industry creates the **least value per dollar invested**. AI hype ≠ AI efficiency.

---

### 💡 Insight 03 — Startup money is genuinely global now

All 5 global regions are within **2% of each other** in total startup funding share.

| Region | Funding Share |
|---|---|
| North America | ~21% |
| Europe | ~21% |
| Asia | ~20% |
| South America | ~19% |
| Africa/Other | ~19% |

> 🌍 The idea that Silicon Valley controls all the startup money? The data says otherwise.

## 📊 Dashboard 2 — Real Funding Deals (2024–25)

> **Dataset:** 25 real companies · $10.7B raised · 7 countries  
> **Notable companies:** SpaceX · OpenAI · Revolut · Anthropic · Byju's · OYO · Razorpay

### 💡 Insight 01 — India raises more per deal than the USA

| Country | Avg. Deal Size |
|---|---|
| 🇺🇸 USA | $403M |
| 🇮🇳 **India** | **$745M** ← nearly 2× |

India's 3 companies in the dataset:
- **Byju's** — $1.2B
- **OYO** — $660M
- **Razorpay** — $375M

> 🇮🇳 Fewer companies, bigger cheques. India punches well above its weight.

---

### 💡 Insight 02 — 60% of all funding went to NON-AI companies

| Category | Share of Funding |
|---|---|
| Non-AI Companies | **60%** |
| AI Companies | 40% |

**Top 5 Deals of 2024–25:**

| # | Company | Industry | Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | SpaceX | Aerospace | $1.5B |
| 2 | Byju's | Education | $1.2B |
| 3 | OpenAI | AI | $1.0B |
| 4 | Anthropic | AI | $0.9B |
| 5 | Revolut | FinTech | $0.8B |

> 💡 2 of the top 3 biggest deals — SpaceX and Byju's — had **nothing to do with AI**.

---

### 💡 Insight 03 — One country raised more than four countries combined

| Region | Total Funding | Share |
|---|---|---|
| 🇺🇸 USA (1 country) | **$6.04B** | 56% |
| 🇮🇳 India (1 country) | $2.24B | 21% |
| 🇪🇺 Europe (UK + France + Germany + Sweden) | $2.19B | 20% |

> 🏆 India ($2.24B) vs Europe ($2.19B) — **India alone beat 4 countries combined** by $50M.

---

### 💡 Insight 04 — The later your funding stage, the more you raise

Startups raise money in rounds. Each round is bigger because the company has proved itself more.

| Stage | Avg. Deal Size |
|---|---|
| Seed | ~$50M (starting out) |
| Series A | ~$150M |
| Series B | ~$350M |
| Series C | ~$650M |
| Series N | ~$1,000M (**20× Seed**) |

> 📈 Surviving long enough to prove yourself is worth more than almost anything else. **Series N is 20× bigger than Seed.**

---

### 💡 Insight 05 — FinTech matched AI in funding with zero media coverage

| Category | Total Raised | Media Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Pure AI Companies | $2,435M | 📢 All the headlines |
| **FinTech Companies** | **$2,425M** | 🔇 No headlines |

> 📰 A $10M difference across billions in global funding. **Nobody wrote a headline about FinTech. The data did not care.**

## 📊 Dashboard 3 — Jobs in the Age of AI (2024–2030)

> **Dataset:** 30,000 job titles · 8 industries · projections to 2030  
> **Cleaned in Excel · Visualized in Power BI**

### 💡 Insight 01 — Who is growing and who is shrinking by 2030

Net job change per industry (2024 → 2030):

| Industry | Net Job Change | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 💚 IT | **+511,824** | ↑ Growing strong |
| 💚 Healthcare | **+403,000** | ↑ Growing strong |
| 💚 Finance | **+120,000** | ↑ Growing |
| 💚 Education | **+45,000** | ↑ Slight growth |
| 🔴 Manufacturing | **-180,000** | ↓ Shrinking |
| 🔴 Retail | **-215,000** | ↓ Shrinking |
| 🔴 Transportation | **-444,000** | ↓ Shrinking hard |

> 🚗 Transportation loses **nearly half a million jobs** — self-driving vehicles, automated delivery, AI logistics. Most people guessed Manufacturing would fall harder. The data disagreed.

---

### 💡 Insight 02 — IT wins on every single measure

IT is the only industry that scores **#1 across all three key metrics at once**:

| Metric | IT Score | Rank |
|---|---|---|
| New Jobs by 2030 | +511,824 | 🥇 #1 |
| Average Salary | $90,942/yr | 🥇 #1 |
| Future Demand | Strongest | 🥇 #1 |

> 🏆 IT is not just surviving AI — it is **powering it**. The safest place to be is the one building the machines.

---

### 💡 Insight 03 — Two numbers that matter right now

| Number | What it Means |
|---|---|
| **4,437** | Jobs at >70% automation risk AND already declining today. Not a future problem — a **right now** problem. |
| **4,520** | Jobs with low automation risk AND growing. Roles requiring human connection, physical unpredictability, or creative thinking. |

> 🛡️ The safe jobs share one thing: **AI cannot replace them**. Human judgment, physical presence, creativity. Build one of those skills.

---

### 💡 Insight 04 — Your salary tells you nothing about your job security

| Industry | Avg. Salary | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 💚 IT | $90,942/yr | Safe ↑ |
| 🔴 Transportation | $89,451/yr | At Risk ↓ |

> **$1,491 per year** separates the safest and most endangered industry. High pay does not mean a safe career. Look at job growth, not just the paycheck.

## 🎯 Recommendations

### For Students & Job Seekers
- ✅ Go toward **IT or Healthcare** — most new jobs, highest pay, lowest risk of disappearing
- ⚠️ If you are in a repetitive job (data entry, routine analysis) — start building new skills now. The 4,437 danger zone jobs are already shrinking
- ✅ Safe careers share one thing: real human judgment, creativity, or physical presence. Build toward that
- ✅ **IT + Healthcare** = the best career combination heading into 2030

### For Businesses (Small to Medium)
- 💡 Do not chase AI just because it is trending — **E-Commerce and HealthTech** quietly deliver better returns
- ✅ If you are in **FinTech**, you have the highest chance of getting acquired — a strong exit strategy
- ⚠️ Transportation and Education businesses need to rethink their models now, not later
- 💡 The salary gap is tiny — retain talent with career growth, skill building, and purpose. Not just pay
